Please Ask AI First

TL;DR

In our healthcare-driven workplace, many general knowledge questions can be answered instantly by AI. Instead of waiting for a teammate to reply in chat, ask AI first. It’s your quickest route to answers for general information, translations, definitions, or other publicly available knowledge. By reserving human input for company-specific or nuanced questions, we maximize productivity and focus.

Why Use AI First?

Imagine this scenario:

2024-12-04 09:00:12 You: "What’s an insurance deductible?"

2024-12-04 09:20:15 Teammate: "It’s the amount a patient pays before insurance kicks in."

Instead of waiting, you could have instantly asked AI:
"What is an insurance deductible?"

AI would have explained it in seconds, maybe with examples or related terms like “co-pay” and “out-of-pocket maximum.”

How to Encourage Smarter Questions

Instead of:
"Hey, what labs are needed for a cholesterol panel?"
Try:
"AI, what labs are included in a cholesterol panel?"

Instead of:
"Does anyone know how to explain a co-pay?"
Try:
"AI, how do I explain a co-pay to someone unfamiliar with insurance?"

If AI doesn’t have the answer or you’re still unsure, you can follow up with a teammate by saying:
"I asked AI about [Question], and it explained [Answer]. However, I’m unsure about [Specific Issue]. Can you clarify?"

Chat Etiquette in an AI-First Culture

If you still need to message someone after trying AI:

  1. Be specific and include all relevant details in your first message.
  2. Provide context to show you’ve already sought an answer independently.
  3. Respect your teammate’s time by framing your question clearly for asynchronous communication.

Example:
"Hi [Name], I checked with AI about fasting for glucose tests, and it said 8-12 hours. For our lab specifically, should patients avoid water too?"

When You Should Ask AI First

Ask AI first when your question is general or informational, such as:

  • Healthcare basics: “How long should a patient fast for a glucose test?”
  • Insurance terms: “What’s the difference between a deductible and a co-pay?”
  • Translations: “How do you say ‘Please provide your ID’ in Spanish?”
  • Procedural how-tos: “How do I explain the difference between HMO and PPO to a patient?”
  • General medical knowledge: “What’s the normal range for a resting heart rate?”

Reserve human interaction for:

  • Internal policies, workflows, or project-specific details.
  • Case-specific questions involving patient details or confidential data.
  • Highly nuanced questions requiring judgment, such as complex patient scenarios.
